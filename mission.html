Our Purpose

The Team's Mission Statement

Navigating campus buildings can be challenging, especially when restroom facilities are inconsistent and difficult to find. Hollister Hall, where men's and women's restrooms alternate by floor, exemplifies a broader issue across Cornell’s campus—many older buildings were not designed to meet modern accessibility and user needs.

Cornell’s current restroom directory focuses only on single-occupant bathrooms and provides locations using coordinates rather than intuitive directions. It often lacks crucial details like accessibility features, amenities, and real-time availability, leaving users without the information needed to make informed choices.

This gap becomes more significant as bathroom preferences and expectations evolve. Modern facilities prioritize full-height partitions, touchless fixtures, personal item storage, and enhanced privacy. Without an effective directory, students waste time and experience unnecessary frustration searching for suitable restrooms.

Our solution addresses these challenges through a dynamic database system linked to an embedded Excel sheet. Users can filter restrooms by criteria such as gender designation, accessibility features, and amenities, transforming how students navigate campus spaces.

Even in its early stages, our prototype surpasses Cornell’s existing directory by enabling searches for women’s, gender-neutral, ADA-compliant, and single-occupancy restrooms. Its flexible structure supports future expansions, ensuring the system grows with user needs.

This directory is more than a tool—it’s a step toward creating a more accessible, user-friendly campus. By providing accurate, up-to-date restroom information, we empower students to plan their time and spaces efficiently, improving both their immediate and long-term campus experiences.
